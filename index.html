1. Introduction

Gas State and Basic Properties

Gas particles have very weak intermolecular forces, so they move freely in all directions at high speed.

Gases have no fixed shape or volume, take the shape of their container, have low density, and are highly compressible.

2. Gas Laws

The Gas Laws

Boyle's Law

P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

At constant T and n, volume is inversely proportional to pressure.

Charles's Law

V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂

At constant P and n, volume is directly proportional to absolute temperature.

Gay-Lussac's Law

P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂

At constant V and n, pressure is directly proportional to temperature.

Avogadro's Law

V₁/n₁ = V₂/n₂

At constant P and T, volume is directly proportional to moles.

At STP, 1 mol of gas occupies 22.4 L.

Combined Gas Law

(P₁V₁)/T₁ = (P₂V₂)/T₂

Use when P, V, and T all change while n stays constant.

3. Ideal Gas

Ideal Gas Equation and Rearranged Forms

Ideal Gas Equation

PV = nRT

Common values of R are 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K) or 8.314 J/(mol·K).

Mass and Density

n = g / M

M = (gRT)/(PV)
D = (PM)/(RT)

Gas density is directly proportional to pressure and inversely proportional to temperature.

Example Problem

If a gas has a mass of 4.40 g in a 2.00 L container at 1.50 atm and 27 °C, convert T = 300 K.

Use M = (gRT)/(PV) to get about 36.12 g/mol.

4 - 5

Partial Pressure, Diffusion, and Kinetic Theory

Dalton's Law

Ptotal = P₁ + P₂ + P₃ + ...

Total pressure equals the sum of the partial pressures of all gases.

Mole Fraction

X₁ = n₁/ntotal

Partial pressure follows mole fraction: P₁ = X₁ · Ptotal.

Graham's Law

r₁/r₂ = √(M₂/M₁)

Lower-molar-mass gases diffuse and effuse faster.

Kinetic Molecular Theory

  • Particles are tiny compared with the container.
  • There are no intermolecular forces in the ideal model.
  • They move in random straight lines.
  • Collisions are perfectly elastic.
  • Ek = 3/2 RT at the same temperature.

Real Gases

Real gases deviate from ideal behavior because particles have finite volume and intermolecular forces.

Closest to ideal: high temperature and low pressure.
